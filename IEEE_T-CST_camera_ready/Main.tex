\begin{abstract}
Cooperative aerial manipulation under unannounced cable severance demands a fault response on the payload-pendulum timescale ($\approx \SI{2}{\second}$), well below the latency of any detection--classification--reconfiguration pipeline. For this class of self-announcing structural transitions, we show that a single decentralized feed-forward identity, $T_i^{\mathrm{ff}} = T_i$, equating each drone's altitude thrust feed-forward to its own measured rope tension, supplies the dominant passive fault-response mechanism. The identity supplies the bounded-jump mechanism required by the hybrid practical input-to-state stability (ISS) certificate, enabling recovery across unannounced severance events without any estimator, detector, or peer communication.

We prove three closed-form guarantees. First, a taut-cable reduction theorem with $O(\delta + \eta_{\max})$ admissibility on a slack-excursion-bounded domain replaces the distributed Kelvin--Voigt rope dynamics with a lumped reduced-order plant. Second, a hybrid practical-ISS theorem yields an explicit recovery envelope with closed-loop Lyapunov decay rate $\lambda$ and per-fault-cycle contraction $\rho \in (0,1)$, both in closed form. Third, a discrete-time gain-selection bound for an optional $L_1$ adaptive altitude augmentation removes the dominant hyper-parameter of the architecture from empirical tuning. Because each drone's closed loop is identical pre- and post-fault and contains no peer-coupled term, the architecture admits a sequence of $F \le N{-}2$ unannounced severances by induction on the fault counter, subject only to the actuator-margin envelope $m_L g/(N{-}F) \le \kappa_{\mathrm{act}} f_{\max}$.

In Drake multibody simulation across a pre-declared six-variant capability sequence and four ablation campaigns at $N=5$, $m_L = \SI{10}{\kilo\gram}$, and \SI{4}{\metre\per\second} Dryden wind, the controller absorbs single and dual unannounced severances within one payload-pendulum period. The feed-forward ablation isolates the identity as the causal mechanism: disabling it inflates cruise RMSE by $34$--$39\,\%$ and post-fault altitude sag by $3.6$--$4.0\times$. Two null findings --- that the model-predictive-control tension-ceiling layer and the post-fault reshape supervisor remain structurally inactive on the demonstrated trajectory family --- support the interpretation that the baseline passive mechanism, rather than conventional active fault-tolerance machinery, explains the observed recovery.
\end{abstract}
