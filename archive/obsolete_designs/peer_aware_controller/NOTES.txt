Peer-aware cascade-PD controller (pre-final generation)
=======================================================
Era: the immediate predecessor of the final fully-local QP controller.

Purpose: Cascade-PD per drone (position → tilt → torque) with one non-
trivial extension: each drone subscribed to an N-vector of peer rope
tensions, computed a smooth `N_alive` count
(`Σᵢ clamp(Tᵢ / T_nom, 0, 1)`), and used it to set an adaptive load-share
feed-forward target `T* = m_L · g / N_alive`. When a cable severed the
failed drone's `Tᵢ` collapsed to zero, `N_alive` dropped, and the
surviving drones automatically lifted a bigger share.

Files:
- decentralized_fault_aware_controller.{cc,h}  Peer-aware controller.
- cable_fault_visualizer.h                     Alternative fault-visualiser
                                               header (never wired in).

Why superseded: the peer-awareness turned out to be redundant. In cruise
the controller was already feeding forward its own measured tension, which
rises automatically when a peer fails (the payload's load redistributes
through the physics of the coupled rope system). The final design
therefore drops the peer-tension input entirely and computes everything
from local observations only, via a local QP.

Replaced by: Research/cpp/src/decentralized_local_controller.cc
