Phase 2 — two-drone decentralized MPC with shared reference
===========================================================
Era: Phase 2 of the research programme.

Purpose: Extension of the Phase-1 MPC to N = 2. Two drones carry the payload
together; the "decentralization" was implemented as a shared-reference
coordination pattern (each drone's controller is an instance of the same
Phase-1 MPC, they share only the payload-trajectory reference).

Files:
- decentralized_mpc_two_drones_test_main.cc   Top-level 2-drone sim harness.
- decentralized_mpc_two_drones_support.cc     Helper system declarations
                                              (cable-severance injector etc.).
- decentralized_mpc_two_drones.h              Public API of the helpers.
- dual_plant_state_splitter.{cc,h}            Two-drone version of the
                                              plant-state splitter.

Why superseded: inherited the fundamental Phase-1 MPC bug (no tracking
gradient in the cost); in addition, output signals were never wired into
the plant's applied-force input. The peer-aware cascade-PD design replaced
it; the fully-local QP design replaces that.
